Privacy Policy

SkyNote · Last updated April 2026

Who we are

SkyNote is a personal notes application. We are committed to protecting your privacy. This policy explains what data we collect, how we use it, and your rights.

What data we collect

  • Email address — used for account creation and sign in only
  • Display name — the username you choose, stored on your profile
  • Note content — titles, body text, and attachments you write
  • Note settings — lock status, password layers, screenshot block preference
  • App passcode — stored locally on your device only, never sent to our servers

How we use your data

  • To provide and sync your notes across devices
  • To allow you to securely share notes with other users via password
  • We do not sell your data to third parties
  • We do not use your data for advertising
  • We do not read your notes

Data storage

Your notes and account data are stored securely using Supabase, a trusted cloud database platform. Data is protected by Row Level Security — only you can access your own notes.

Attachments are stored as base64 within the note data. Images embedded in notes are stored inline.

Shared notes

When you lock a note and share its password, the locked note's title and content become readable by anyone who knows the correct password and your username. You control this entirely — locking and unlocking is always your choice.

Screenshot protection

When you enable the "Block screenshots" option on a note, SkyNote applies technical measures to prevent screenshots while a viewer is reading that note. This feature uses device-level APIs where available. We cannot guarantee 100% prevention on all devices.

Your rights

  • You can delete any note at any time
  • You can sign out and your session is immediately terminated
  • To permanently delete your account and all data, contact us
  • You can export your notes by copying content from the app

Children's privacy

SkyNote is not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect data from children under 13. If you believe a child has provided us with personal data, please contact us.

Changes to this policy

We may update this policy occasionally. We will notify you of significant changes by updating the date at the top of this page.

Contact

If you have any questions about this privacy policy or your data, please open an issue on our GitHub repository.
